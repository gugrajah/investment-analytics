### **Internal Corporate & Operational Efficiency Metrics at the PIC**

The PIC sits at an interesting intersection: it's a state-owned entity governed by the PFMA, but it operates like a sophisticated asset manager. That dual identity shapes which metrics matter internally.

---

#### **Finance & Budget Management**

* **Operational expenditure vs. budget** (monthly/quarterly variance)  
* **Revenue from management fees** per client fund (GEPF, UIF, CC Fund, etc.)  
* **Cost-to-income ratio** — a standard efficiency measure for asset managers  
* **Irregular, unauthorised & wasteful expenditure** — this is a statutory PFMA requirement and the Auditor-General tracks unauthorised, irregular, and wasteful expenditure as part of the PIC's audit outcomes, so it's almost certainly reported internally too [National Government of South Africa](https://nationalgovernment.co.za/units/financial/149/public-investment-corporation-soc-ltd-pic)  
* **Audit findings tracker** — the PIC has received an unqualified audit opinion for seven consecutive years, meaning there's likely a management action plan dashboard to maintain that record [Pic](https://www.pic.gov.za/DocAnnualReports1/PIC%202025%20Integrated%20Annual%20Report.pdf)

---

#### **Human Capital & Workforce**

* **Headcount by division** and vacancy rate — the PIC has approximately 403 employees, a lean organisation for the scale of assets managed [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_Investment_Corporation)  
* **Employee turnover rate** and time-to-fill vacancies  
* **Training hours per employee** and skills development spend (a B-BBEE reporting requirement)  
* **Employment Equity compliance** against targets (race, gender, disability representation by level)  
* **Performance review completion rate**

---

#### **Governance & Compliance**

* **Policy compliance rate** — percentage of decisions that followed approved mandates and delegation of authority  
* **Board and committee meeting attendance/quorum tracking**  
* **Declaration of interests** — submission completion rate across staff  
* **Internal audit findings** (open vs. closed, by risk rating and division)  
* **Regulatory submission timeliness** — PFMA reports, parliamentary submissions, FSCA disclosures

---

#### **Investment Operations (Operational side, not portfolio returns)**

* **Client mandate compliance rate** — are investment decisions within the bounds of each client's approved mandate?  
* **Trade settlement accuracy and turnaround time**  
* **Proxy voting execution rate** — the PIC is a major shareholder; tracking whether votes were cast on time is operational  
* **External manager reporting compliance** — the PIC allocates a minimum of 60% of approved funds to external managers, so monitoring their reporting submissions is a real operational task [Pic](https://www.pic.gov.za/DocAnnualReports1/PIC%20Integrated%20Annual%20Report%202022.pdf)  
* **BEE brokerage spend tracking** — the PIC actively tracks brokerage spend directed to Level 1–4 BEE brokers and firms with at least 51% HDI ownership [Pic](https://www.pic.gov.za/DocAnnualReports1/PIC%20Integrated%20Annual%20Report%202022.pdf)

---

#### **Transformation & ESG Reporting**

* **B-BBEE scorecard progress** across the five elements  
* **ESG integration rate** in new investment decisions  
* **Developmental impact metrics** — jobs created/supported by Isibaya and listed investments  
* **Gender and HDI representation** in investment portfolios and board positions of investees

---

#### **IT & Project Delivery**

* **System uptime and availability** for investment management platforms  
* **Project milestone adherence** (on-time, on-budget delivery rates)  
* **Data quality metrics** — given the PIC's data teams are actively restructuring and migrating historic information, a data completeness/quality dashboard is very likely a live need right now [Pic](https://www.pic.gov.za/)  
* **Helpdesk ticket resolution time** by category and SLA breach rate

Given the Power BI developer framing, you'd be well served to anchor your answers around two things the PIC visibly cares about: **governance and accountability** (PFMA compliance, clean audits, internal control monitoring) and **transformation reporting** (B-BBEE, HDI representation, developmental impact). These are the areas where a well-designed dashboard would deliver the most visible institutional value — and they're also the areas where Excel-based manual reporting is most likely still happening.

**Listed Investments & Market Risk Metrics** For its listed equities, fixed-income portfolios, and property investments, the PIC utilizes standard financial and risk metrics to track performance against benchmarks:

* **Sharpe Ratio:** Measures the portfolio's excess return per unit of total risk, assessing risk-adjusted returns.  
* **Tracking Error:** Quantifies the active risk or volatility of the portfolio relative to its specific benchmark, with the PIC targeting strict maximum tracking error limits.  
* **Value at Risk (VaR):** Estimates the maximum potential loss of a portfolio within a specified confidence level over a given time horizon, which helps evaluate market risk concentrations.  
* **Total Return (Capital and Income):** Used specifically for measuring property investments against customised industry benchmarks like the MSCI RE (formerly IPD).

**Unlisted Investments & Private Credit Metrics** For its unlisted developmental portfolio (such as the Isibaya Fund), the PIC tracks metrics that assess the performance of illiquid assets:

* **Internal Rate of Return (IRR):** The discount rate at which the net present value of all cash flows (capital calls, distributions, and current unrealized valuation) equals zero. This is used to flag underperforming assets, such as those that hit a \-100% IRR.  
* **Multiple on Invested Capital (MOIC):** Evaluates the total value generated (realized cash distributions plus unrealized current value) relative to the paid-in capital.  
* **Distributions to Paid-In Capital (DPI):** Measures the proportion of invested capital that has been successfully returned to clients as cash.
